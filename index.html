See It in Action

Beautiful, intuitive interface designed for macOS

Your Desktop Bookshelf

TopShelf floats on your desktop, always accessible. Move it anywhere by clicking and dragging. Each book is a shortcut to your most important files, folders, or apps.

  • Drag to move anywhere on your screen
  • Click the pencil button to edit bookshelf
  • Click the close button to minimize
  • Double-click any book to activate it

Manage Your Bookshelf

Edit mode gives you complete control over your bookshelf. Reorder books with up/down arrows, edit titles and settings, or remove books you no longer need. The clean list view shows all your books at a glance with their icons and shortcuts.

  • Drag-free reordering with arrow buttons
  • Quick edit and delete options for each book
  • Visual icons show book types at a glance
  • Add unlimited books to your collection

Add Books with Custom Styles

Create new books with beautiful, customizable spines. Choose from 7 AI-generated textures including aged leather and vintage cloth. Pick from three sizes to match your needs, and add your own title that displays vertically on the spine.

  • 7 realistic spine textures (leather & cloth)
  • 3 size options: Small, Medium, Large
  • Custom titles with automatic text scaling
  • Browse button for easy file/folder selection

Customize Every Detail

Fine-tune every aspect of your books. Change the title, switch between file/folder/app/URL types, adjust the size, or pick a different spine style. Your bookshelf evolves with your needs - update any book anytime.

  • Switch between File, Folder, App, or URL types
  • Change spine style and size anytime
  • Update book titles as your projects evolve
  • Visual spine preview before saving

Menu Bar Control

Access TopShelf from your menu bar anytime. The bookshelf icon gives you quick control over the app without leaving your current work.

  • Show/hide the bookshelf with Cmd+B
  • Edit your bookshelf with Cmd+E
  • Keep bookshelf always on top of other windows
  • Quick access to quit (Cmd+Q)
